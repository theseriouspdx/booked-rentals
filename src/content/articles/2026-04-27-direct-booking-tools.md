Every platform booking comes with a cost: commission fees that run 3% to 15% depending on the channel, guest data you can't retain, and a relationship that lives entirely inside someone else's app. After years of hosting across Airbnb, VRBO, and Booking.com, we've become convinced that a direct booking channel isn't optional if you're serious about building a sustainable rental business. It's a hedge, a margin improvement, and a guest relationship tool all at once.

This doesn't mean abandoning platforms entirely. Most hosts get the bulk of their volume from Airbnb and VRBO, and that's fine. The goal is to give repeat guests somewhere to book directly, capture email addresses, and keep more of what you earn on those stays.

## What to Look for in a Direct Booking Tool

Not all direct booking tools are created equal. Before you commit to one, think through these factors:

**Custom domain support.** Your direct booking site should live at your own domain, not a subdomain of your tool provider's site. A URL like `yourvilla.com` signals professionalism; a URL like `yourproperty.hospitable.com` is fine to start but shouldn't be the end state.

**Template quality.** You're not a web designer, and you shouldn't have to be. Look for tools with well-designed, mobile-friendly templates that let your photos carry the page. A great property can look mediocre with a bad template.

**Booking engine.** The whole point is direct bookings, so the booking engine needs to handle availability calendars, payment processing, and confirmation emails reliably. Bonus if it syncs with your channel manager so you're not managing a separate calendar.

**Channel manager compatibility.** If you're already using a channel manager (Hospitable, Hostaway, Guesty, etc.), your direct booking site should connect to it. You want one source of truth for pricing and availability, not two calendars to maintain.

**Price.** Some tools are free or included with your channel manager. Others are a meaningful monthly subscription. Factor in whether the commission savings from direct bookings actually justify a paid tool's cost at your current booking volume.

## Hospitable's Direct Booking Site Builder

Hospitable is primarily known as a channel manager and automation platform -- it handles messaging, pricing sync, and review requests across platforms. But it also includes a direct booking site builder, and for hosts already on Hospitable, this is worth paying attention to.

The site builder is free, which is the first thing to note. You get a professional-looking direct booking page with your property details, photos, and a live booking engine, at no additional cost beyond your existing Hospitable subscription. For hosts who haven't built a direct booking channel yet, this is a zero-friction starting point.

Hospitable's site builder supports custom domains, which means you can connect your own URL rather than using a Hospitable subdomain. There are multiple templates to choose from, so you have some flexibility on the look. The booking engine connects directly to your Hospitable-managed availability and pricing, so there's no extra calendar management required.

If you're not yet on Hospitable, their free tier is also worth evaluating as a low-barrier entry point -- you get enough to test whether direct bookings are generating any volume before committing to a paid tool.

The honest limitation is that Hospitable's site builder is relatively simple compared to dedicated direct booking website tools. You won't get a multi-property portfolio page, advanced SEO controls, or deep customization. For a single property or a small portfolio where you want something functional without a project, it's a strong choice. For hosts who want more design control or a more polished brand presence, you may eventually outgrow it.

## Other Options to Consider

If you want more than what your channel manager offers, a few tools come up consistently:

**Lodgify** is a dedicated direct booking website platform with good templates and a built-in booking engine. It's a paid tool, but it's purpose-built for STR hosts.

**Hostaway** includes a direct booking website feature for subscribers, similar to Hospitable's approach -- useful if you're already on that platform.

**WordPress or Squarespace with a booking plugin** gives you maximum flexibility and design control but requires more setup and ongoing maintenance. Worth it if you have the time or a developer; not worth it if you don't.

## The Bottom Line

If you're already using Hospitable, their free site builder is the obvious first move. Set it up in an afternoon, connect your custom domain, and start including the link in your post-stay messages to repeat guests. You'll find out quickly whether direct bookings are a meaningful revenue stream for your market.

If you're evaluating tools from scratch, start with what your channel manager already offers before paying for something separate. Most channel managers have some direct booking capability built in, and using it costs nothing extra. Only move to a dedicated tool once you've established that direct bookings are generating enough volume to justify it.

The goal isn't to replace Airbnb. It's to own a relationship with your best guests so the next time they want to book, they have another option -- one that costs them less in service fees and costs you less in commissions.
