Airbnb markets AirCover as a million-dollar safety net for hosts. The reality is more nuanced. After filing multiple claims over several years of hosting, here's what you actually need to know about how the process works, what gets approved, and what falls through the cracks.

## What AirCover Actually Covers

AirCover for Hosts provides protection against guest-caused damage to your property, unexpected cleaning costs that go beyond normal wear and tear, and income loss if you need to cancel future bookings due to damage. On paper, it covers up to $3 million in damage. In practice, the approval process is where things get complicated.

## The Documentation Game

The single biggest factor in whether your claim gets paid is documentation. Before every guest checks in, you need timestamped photos of every room, every surface, every item of value. After checkout, do the same walkthrough. The moment you find damage, photograph it immediately and submit your claim within the platform's deadline — typically 14 days or before the next guest checks in, whichever comes first. Miss that window and you're out of luck.

## What Doesn't Get Covered

Normal wear and tear is the gray area where most disputes happen. A stain on a five-year-old couch? Probably wear and tear. A hole punched in a wall? Clear damage. But a scratched hardwood floor from furniture being dragged? That's where you'll spend hours on support chat. Pre-existing conditions that weren't documented will always be denied, which is why that pre-guest photo walkthrough isn't optional.
