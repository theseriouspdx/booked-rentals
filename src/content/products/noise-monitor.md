Noise complaints from neighbors are one of the fastest ways to lose your short-term rental permit. A noise monitor gives you real-time alerts when sound levels exceed your threshold, without recording conversations or violating guest privacy.

## Privacy-First Monitoring

The key distinction with noise monitors is that they measure decibel levels only — no audio is recorded, no conversations are captured. This is important both legally and for guest trust. You must disclose the device in your listing (most platforms require this), but guests are generally comfortable once they understand it only detects volume, not content.

## How We Use It

We set our threshold at 80 decibels sustained for more than 5 minutes. That filters out normal activity like a movie at full volume or a group dinner conversation while catching actual parties or disturbances. When triggered, we get a push notification and can message the guest with a friendly reminder about quiet hours.
