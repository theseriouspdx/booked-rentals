Every experienced host has stories. The kind you share in hosting forums at midnight, wondering if anyone else has dealt with something this absurd. After years of hosting, here are seven real scenarios and the exact playbooks that got us through each one.

## The Unauthorized Party

You get a noise alert at 11 PM on a Saturday. Your two-guest booking now has twenty cars in the driveway. Step one: message the guest immediately through the platform so there's a written record. Step two: if they don't respond within 15 minutes, call them. Step three: if the party continues, contact the platform's emergency line and local non-emergency police. Document everything with timestamps. This is grounds for immediate removal and a full claim.

## The Extended Checkout

Checkout was at 11 AM. It's now 1 PM, your cleaner is waiting, and the guest isn't responding to messages. This one requires patience and documentation. Message through the platform, call the guest, and give a firm but polite deadline. If they still haven't left by the time your next guest is affected, escalate to the platform. Most importantly, make sure your house rules clearly state late checkout fees — it gives you leverage.

## The Damage Denier

They broke something and claim it was already broken. This is why pre-stay documentation is everything. If you have timestamped photos from before check-in showing the item intact, your claim is straightforward. Without them, it becomes a he-said-she-said that the platform will usually resolve in the guest's favor.
