Short-term rental regulation is evolving rapidly across the United States. What was legal last year may require a new permit this year, and enforcement is ramping up in many markets. We're tracking the changes that matter most to hosts.

## The Trend Toward Registration

The most common regulatory change in 2026 is mandatory host registration. Cities that previously had a hands-off approach are now requiring hosts to register their properties, pay annual fees, and display registration numbers on their listings. Non-compliance penalties are getting steeper — some markets are now issuing fines per night of unauthorized operation rather than one-time penalties.

## Insurance Requirements

Several states are now requiring hosts to carry specific short-term rental insurance beyond standard homeowner's policies. This is separate from platform-provided coverage like AirCover. The requirements vary widely — some markets accept a rider on your existing policy, while others require a standalone STR insurance product. Check your local requirements before assuming your current coverage is sufficient.
