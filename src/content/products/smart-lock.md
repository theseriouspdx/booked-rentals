If there's one upgrade that has saved us more time and headaches than any other, it's switching to a smart lock. Before installing one, check-in was a logistical nightmare — coordinating key handoffs, dealing with lost keys, and fielding frantic messages from guests who couldn't figure out the lockbox.

## Why Keyless Entry Changes Everything

With a smart lock, every guest gets a unique code that activates at check-in and expires at checkout. No key handoffs, no lockbox combinations to change, no emergency locksmith calls. The lock integrates with most property management systems, so codes are generated and sent automatically as part of the booking confirmation.

## What to Look For

The most important features are reliability, battery life, and app integration. You want a lock that works even when WiFi is down (offline code support), lasts at least 6 months on a battery charge, and gives you real-time notifications when the door is locked or unlocked. Auto-lock is also essential — guests forget to lock up, and you don't want your property sitting open overnight.
